Dashboard Philosophy — Who Is This For?

The #1 reason dashboards fail is trying to serve everyone. Let's pick a primary audience.

A

Ops Engineer — "Is everything running?"

Pipeline health, error rates, failing banks, active crawls. Real-time focus. Think: Datadog/Grafana.

Focus

  • Crawl success/failure by bank
  • Error breakdown & alerts
  • Strategy health & anti-bot signals
  • Pipeline throughput metrics
B

Product/Business — "What rates are moving?"

Rate trends, competitive intelligence, data quality scores. Analytical focus. Think: Bloomberg terminal lite.

Focus

  • Rate trends over time (KPR, KPA, etc.)
  • Bank-by-bank rate comparison
  • Data completeness & confidence
  • New programs discovered
C

Hybrid — "Health + Intelligence" (Recommended)

Top section = system health snapshot (green/red at a glance). Bottom section = rate intelligence & data quality. The Overview should answer: "Is the system healthy AND what did we learn today?"

Focus

  • Health strip: pipeline status, success rate, errors
  • Intelligence: rate heatmap with REAL data, trends
  • Quality: completeness scores, confidence
  • Activity: meaningful events only (no pings)
